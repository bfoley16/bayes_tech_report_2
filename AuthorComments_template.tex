\section*{Summary of technical report and contributions}

Read the following instructions, and comment them out before submitting your referee report: \\

\noindent It is common when conducting a review to provide a summary of the paper / report \textit{as you yourself understood it}, so that the authors can determine whether or not the main points they were trying to get across were clearly explained. For example, if I wrote a paper about a new statistical method for estimating under-5 mortality and my referees summarized my paper as being about life expectancy, this would indicate to me that my writing was unfocused and not clear enough to effectively get my point across. \\

\noindent While you all recieved the same prompt for the technical report, write your summary of your peer's TR \textit{as you understood it}. That means, even if your understanding of the problem was different, summarize it as it comes across in \textit{their} written work. Some things to consider mentioning:

\begin{itemize}
    \item What is the problem they are trying to address?
    \item What methodology did they use to address it? This includes statistical modeling choices, data cleaning choices that impact results (if any), and what computational methods they may have used to address their question.
    \item What were their main findings? Were there limitations to their analysis? If so, did they propose ways these could be addressed in future analyses?
\end{itemize}
